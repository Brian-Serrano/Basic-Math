Subtraction is another fundamental arithmetic operation in mathematics that involves finding the difference between two numbers. The symbol used to represent subtraction is "-".

To perform subtraction, you subtract one number (the subtrahend) from another number (the minuend) to find the result (the difference). For example, to subtract 3 from 7, you would write:

  • 7 - 3 = 4

Here are some more examples of subtraction:

  • 10 - 5 = 5
  • 15 - 8 = 7
  • 20 - 12 = 8
  • 9 - 3 = 6
  • 30 - 25 = 5

Subtraction can also involve negative numbers. When subtracting a negative number, it is equivalent to adding the positive value of that number. For example:

  • 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8
  • 10 - (-7) = 10 + 7 = 17
  • (-2) - (-8) = (-2) + 8 = 6

When subtracting fractions, you need to find a common denominator before subtracting the numerators. For example:

  • 12 - 14 = 24 - 14 = 14
  • 34 - 12 = 68 - 48 = 28 = 14
  • 23 - 13 = 13

Subtraction with decimals is similar to subtraction with whole numbers. Line up the decimal points and subtract the numbers as you normally would. For example:

  • 3.5 - 1.2 = 2.3
  • 6.8 - 2.1 = 4.7
  • 15.2 - 7.6 = 7.6

These are just a few examples of subtraction in math. It is a foundational operation that is used in various mathematical contexts, such as solving equations, measuring differences, and finding the change in quantities.
