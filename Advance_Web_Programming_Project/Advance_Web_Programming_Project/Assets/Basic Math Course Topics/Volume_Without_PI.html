Let's discuss finding the volume of a cube, rectangular prism, and pyramid, and provide examples:

  1. Cube:

    A cube is a three-dimensional shape with six equal square faces. To find the volume of a cube, you can use the formula:

    • Volume of a cube = side length3

    Example:

    Suppose we have a cube with a side length of 5 units. The volume of this cube would be:

    • Volume = 53 = 125 cubic units

    So, the volume of the cube is 125 cubic units.

  2. Rectangular Prism:

    A rectangular prism, also known as a rectangular cuboid, is a three-dimensional shape with six rectangular faces. To find the volume of a rectangular prism, you can use the formula:

    • Volume of a rectangular prism = length * width * height

    Example:

    Consider a rectangular prism with a length of 8 units, a width of 6 units, and a height of 4 units. The volume of this rectangular prism would be:

    • Volume = 8 * 6 * 4 = 192 cubic units

    So, the volume of the rectangular prism is 192 cubic units.

  3. Pyramid:

    A pyramid is a three-dimensional shape with a polygonal base and triangular faces converging to a single point called the apex. To find the volume of a pyramid, you can use the formula:

    • Volume of a pyramid = base area * height3

    Example:

    Suppose we have a pyramid with a base area of 36 square units and a height of 10 units. The volume of this pyramid would be:

    • Volume = 36 * 103 = 120 cubic units

    So, the volume of the pyramid is 120 cubic units.

These formulas and examples can help you find the volume of three-dimensional shapes such as a cube, rectangular prism, and pyramid. Remember to use the appropriate formula based on the given shape and its properties.
