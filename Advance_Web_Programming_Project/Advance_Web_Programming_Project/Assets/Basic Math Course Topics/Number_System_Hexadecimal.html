I'll discuss the conversion from hexadecimal to the other three base systems: decimal, binary, and octal.

  1. Conversion from Hexadecimal to Decimal (Base-16 to Base-10):

    To convert a hexadecimal number to decimal, you multiply each digit of the hexadecimal number by the corresponding power of 16 and then sum up the results.

    Example:

    Let's convert the hexadecimal number 3A7 to decimal.

    • Step 1: Assign powers of 16 to each digit from right to left:

      3 * 162 + A * 161 + 7 * 160

    • Step 2: Perform the calculations:

      3 * 256 + 10 * 16 + 7 * 1 = 768 + 160 + 7 = 935

    Therefore, the hexadecimal number 3A7 is equivalent to the decimal number 935.

  2. Conversion from Hexadecimal to Binary (Base-16 to Base-2):

    To convert a hexadecimal number to binary, you replace each hexadecimal digit with its equivalent 4-digit binary representation.

    Example:

    Let's convert the hexadecimal number 1E9 to binary.

    • Replace each hexadecimal digit with its equivalent 4-digit binary representation:

      1 (hexadecimal) = 0001 (binary)

      E (hexadecimal) = 1110 (binary)

      9 (hexadecimal) = 1001 (binary)

    Therefore, the hexadecimal number 1E9 is equivalent to the binary number 000111101001.

  3. Conversion from Hexadecimal to Octal (Base-16 to Base-8):

    To convert a hexadecimal number to octal, you first convert the hexadecimal number to binary and then convert the binary number to octal.

    Example:

    Let's convert the hexadecimal number D42 to octal.

    • Step 1: Convert the hexadecimal number to binary:

      D (hexadecimal) = 1101 (binary)

      4 (hexadecimal) = 0100 (binary)

      2 (hexadecimal) = 0010 (binary)

      So, the hexadecimal number D42 is equivalent to the binary number 11010010010.

    • Step 2: Convert the binary number to octal:

      11010010010 (binary) = 6242 (octal)

    Therefore, the hexadecimal number D42 is equivalent to the octal number 6242.

These examples demonstrate how to convert hexadecimal numbers to decimal, binary, and octal. Understanding these conversions is important in computer systems, digital electronics, and programming languages, where hexadecimal numbers are commonly used.
