The area of a three-sided shape is a measure of the region enclosed by its three sides. The formulas to find the area of different three-sided shapes are as follows:

  1. Right Triangle:

    A right triangle is a triangle with one 90-degree angle. To find the area of a right triangle, you multiply the lengths of the two perpendicular sides (legs) and divide the result by 2.

    • Area of a right triangle = base * height2

    Example:

    Let's say we have a right triangle with a base of 4 units and a height of 6 units. The area of this right triangle would be:

    • Area = 4 * 62 = 242 = 12 square units

    So, the area of the right triangle is 12 square units.

  2. Equilateral Triangle:

    An equilateral triangle is a triangle with all sides of equal length. To find the area of an equilateral triangle, you can use the formula based on the side length.

    • Area of an equilateral triangle = side length2 * √34

    Example:

    Consider an equilateral triangle with a side length of 5 units. The area of this equilateral triangle would be:

    • Area = 52 * √34 ≈ 10.825 square units

    So, the area of the equilateral triangle is approximately 10.825 square units.

  3. Isosceles or Scalene Triangle:

    An isosceles triangle is a triangle with two sides of equal length, while a scalene triangle has no equal sides. To find the area of an isosceles or scalene triangle, you can use the formula based on the base and height.

    • Area of an isosceles or scalene triangle = base * height2

    Example:

    Suppose we have an isosceles or scalene triangle with a base of 8 units and a height of 5 units. The area of this triangle would be:

    • Area = 8 * 52 = 402 = 20 square units

    So, the area of the isosceles or scalene triangle is 20 square units.

These formulas and examples can help you find the area of three-sided shapes such as a right triangle, equilateral triangle, and isosceles or scalene triangle. Remember to use the appropriate formula based on the given shape and its properties.
