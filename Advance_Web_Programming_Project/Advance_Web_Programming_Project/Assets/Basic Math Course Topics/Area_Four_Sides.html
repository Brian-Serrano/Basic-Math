The area of a four-sided shape is a measure of the region enclosed by its four sides. The formulas to find the area of different four-sided shapes are as follows:

  1. Square:

    A square is a four-sided polygon with all sides of equal length. To find the area of a square, you multiply the length of one side by itself.

    • Area of a square = side length * side length = side length2

    Example:

    Let's say we have a square with a side length of 5 units. The area of this square would be:

    • Area = 5 * 5 = 25 square units

    So, the area of the square is 25 square units.

  2. Rectangle:

    A rectangle is a four-sided polygon with opposite sides of equal length. To find the area of a rectangle, you multiply the length of one side (length) by the length of the adjacent side (width).

    • Area of a rectangle = length * width

    Example:

    Consider a rectangle with a length of 8 units and a width of 6 units. The area of this rectangle would be:

    • Area = 8 * 6 = 48 square units

    So, the area of the rectangle is 48 square units.

  3. Trapezoid:

    A trapezoid is a four-sided polygon with one pair of parallel sides. To find the area of a trapezoid, you multiply the average of the lengths of the parallel sides (base1 and base2) by the height (distance between the parallel sides).

    • Area of a trapezoid = (base1 + base2) * height2

    Example:

    Suppose we have a trapezoid with base1 of 6 units, base2 of 10 units, and a height of 5 units. The area of this trapezoid would be:

    • Area = (6 + 10) * 52 = 16 * 52 = 40 square units

    So, the area of the trapezoid is 40 square units.

  4. Parallelogram:

    A parallelogram is a four-sided polygon with opposite sides that are parallel and equal in length. To find the area of a parallelogram, you multiply the length of the base by the height.

    • Area of a parallelogram = base * height

    Example:

    Consider a parallelogram with a base length of 7 units and a height of 9 units. The area of this parallelogram would be:

    • Area = 7 * 9 = 63 square units

    So, the area of the parallelogram is 63 square units.

These formulas and examples can help you find the area of four-sided shapes such as a square, rectangle, trapezoid, and parallelogram. Remember to use the appropriate formula based on the given shape and its properties.
