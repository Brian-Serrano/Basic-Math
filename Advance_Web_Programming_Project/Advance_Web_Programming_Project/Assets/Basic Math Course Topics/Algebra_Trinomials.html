A Perfect Square Trinomial is a special algebraic expression that can be factored into the square of a binomial. It follows a specific pattern, and recognizing this pattern can help simplify the expression. The general form of a Perfect Square Trinomial is:

  • a2 + 2ab + b2 = (a + b)2

where 'a' and 'b' can be any real numbers or algebraic expressions. Here's how you can identify and use Perfect Square Trinomials:

  1. Identify the expression in the form a2 + 2ab + b2.
  2. Check if the first and last terms are perfect squares.
    • The first term should be the square of some value, and the last term should also be the square of some value.
  3. Check if the middle term is twice the product of the square roots of the first and last terms.
    • The middle term should be twice the product of the square roots of the first and last terms.
  4. Express the trinomial as the square of a binomial:
    • Write the square root of the first term as the first term of the binomial.
    • Write the square root of the last term as the second term of the binomial.
    • Ensure that the sign between the two terms matches the sign in the original trinomial.
