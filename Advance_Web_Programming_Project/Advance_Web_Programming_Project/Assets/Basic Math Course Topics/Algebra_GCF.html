Factoring out the Greatest Common Factor (GCF) is a process used in algebra to simplify expressions by finding the largest common factor of all the terms and factoring it out. This helps in reducing the complexity of the expression and identifying common factors or simplifying further. Here's how you can factor out the GCF in algebra:

  1. Identify the terms in the expression.
    • Look for any common factors that appear in all the terms.
  2. Determine the GCF of the terms.
    • Find the largest factor that is common to all the terms. This is the GCF.
  3. Write the GCF outside parentheses.
    • Place the GCF outside parentheses.
  4. Divide each term by the GCF.
    • Divide each term by the GCF and write the result inside the parentheses.

Example 1:

Factor out the GCF of the expression 18x3 - 12x2 + 6x.

  • Step 1: Identify the terms: 18x3, -12x2, 6x.
  • Step 2: Determine the GCF: The common factors of the coefficients (18, -12, 6) are 6 and the common factors of the variables (x3, x2, x) is x.
    • GCF = 6x.
  • Step 3: Write the GCF outside parentheses: 6x
  • Step 4: Divide each term by the GCF and write the result inside the parentheses:
    • 18x3 ÷ (6x) = 3x2
    • -12x2 ÷ (6x) = -2x
    • 6x ÷ (6x) = 1

The factored expression is: 6x(3x2 - 2x + 1).

Example 2:

Factor out the GCF of the expression 15a2b - 25ab2 + 10a2b3.

  • Step 1: Identify the terms: 15a2b, -25ab2, 10a2b3.
  • Step 2: Determine the GCF: The common factors of the coefficients (15, -25, 10) are 5, and the common factors of the variables (a2, ab, a2b3) are a2b.
    • GCF = 5a2b.
  • Step 3: Write the GCF outside parentheses: 5a2b
  • Step 4: Divide each term by the GCF and write the result inside the parentheses:
    • 15a2b ÷ (5a2b) = 3
    • -25ab2 ÷ (5a2b) = -5b
    • 10a2b3 ÷ (5a2b) = 2b2

The factored expression is: 5a2b(3 - 5b + 2b2).

By factoring out the GCF, you simplify the expression and make it easier to work with. It helps in identifying common factors, reducing complexity, and further simplifying the expression if needed.
