Factoring by grouping is a technique used in algebra to factorize expressions with four or more terms by grouping them into pairs. This method is particularly useful when there is a common factor in each pair. Here's how you can use the factoring by grouping method:

  1. Group the terms in the expression into pairs.
    • Look for pairs of terms that have a common factor.
  2. Factor out the common factor from each pair.
    • For each pair, factor out the greatest common factor of the terms in the pair.
  3. Look for a common factor in the resulting binomials.
    • Look for a common factor between the binomials obtained from step 2.
  4. Factor out the common factor from the binomials.
    • Factor out the greatest common factor of the binomials.

Let's look at an example:

Example:

Factor the expression 3ab + 6a - 4b - 8.

  • Step 1: Group the terms into pairs.

    (3ab + 6a) - (4b + 8)

  • Step 2: Factor out the common factor from each pair.

    3a(b + 2) - 4(b + 2)

  • Step 3: Look for a common factor in the resulting binomials.

    Notice that (b + 2) is a common factor.

  • Step 4: Factor out the common factor from the binomials.

    (b + 2)(3a - 4)

So, the expression 3ab + 6a - 4b - 8 can be factored as (b + 2)(3a - 4) using the factoring by grouping method.

Factoring by grouping is a useful technique when dealing with expressions with multiple terms. It allows us to simplify and factorize expressions, making them easier to work with and solve.
