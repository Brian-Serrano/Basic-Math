Let's discuss finding the volume of a cylinder, sphere, and cone, and provide examples:

  1. Cylinder:

    A cylinder is a three-dimensional shape with two parallel circular bases connected by a curved surface. To find the volume of a cylinder, you can use the formula:

    • Volume of a cylinder = π * radius2 * height

    Example:

    Suppose we have a cylinder with a radius of 3 units and a height of 8 units. The volume of this cylinder would be:

    • Volume = π * 32 * 8 = 72π cubic units (approximately 226.195 cubic units)

    So, the volume of the cylinder is approximately 72π cubic units or 226.195 cubic units.

  2. Sphere:

    A sphere is a three-dimensional shape with all points on its surface equidistant from its center. To find the volume of a sphere, you can use the formula:

    • Volume of a sphere = 43 * π * radius3

    Example:

    Consider a sphere with a radius of 5 units. The volume of this sphere would be:

    • Volume = 43 * π * 53 = 43 * π * 125 = 500π cubic units (approximately 1570.796 cubic units)

    So, the volume of the sphere is approximately 500π cubic units or 1570.796 cubic units.

  3. Cone:

    A cone is a three-dimensional shape with a circular base and a pointed top. To find the volume of a cone, you can use the formula:

    • Volume of a cone = 13 * π * radius2 * height

    Example:

    Suppose we have a cone with a radius of 4 units and a height of 6 units. The volume of this cone would be:

    • Volume = 13 * π * 42 * 6 = 13 * π * 16 * 6 = 32π cubic units (approximately 100.531 cubic units)

    So, the volume of the cone is approximately 32π cubic units or 100.531 cubic units.

These formulas and examples can help you find the volume of three-dimensional shapes such as a cylinder, sphere, and cone. Remember to use the appropriate formula based on the given shape and its properties.
