The perimeter of a three-sided shape is the total length of its three sides. The formulas to find the perimeter of different three-sided shapes are as follows:

  1. Right Triangle:

    A right triangle is a triangle with one 90-degree angle. To find the perimeter of a right triangle, you add the lengths of all three sides.

    • Perimeter of a right triangle = side A + side B + hypotenuse

    Example:

    Let's say we have a right triangle with side lengths of 3 units, 4 units, and 5 units. The perimeter of this right triangle would be:

    • Perimeter = 3 + 4 + 5 = 12 units

    So, the perimeter of the right triangle is 12 units.

  2. Equilateral Triangle:

    An equilateral triangle is a triangle with all sides of equal length. To find the perimeter of an equilateral triangle, you simply multiply the length of one side by 3.

    • Perimeter of an equilateral triangle = 3 * side length

    Example:

    Consider an equilateral triangle with a side length of 6 units. The perimeter of this equilateral triangle would be:

    • Perimeter = 3 * 6 = 18 units

    So, the perimeter of the equilateral triangle is 18 units.

  3. Isosceles or Scalene Triangle:

    An isosceles triangle is a triangle with two sides of equal length, while a scalene triangle has no equal sides. To find the perimeter of an isosceles or scalene triangle, you add the lengths of all three sides.

    • Perimeter of an isosceles or scalene triangle = side A + side B + side C

    Example:

    Suppose we have an isosceles or scalene triangle with side lengths of 5 units, 7 units, and 6 units. The perimeter of this triangle would be:

    • Perimeter = 5 + 7 + 6 = 18 units

    So, the perimeter of the isosceles or scalene triangle is 18 units.

These formulas and examples can help you find the perimeter of three-sided shapes such as a right triangle, equilateral triangle, and isosceles or scalene triangle. Remember to use the appropriate formula based on the given shape and its properties.
