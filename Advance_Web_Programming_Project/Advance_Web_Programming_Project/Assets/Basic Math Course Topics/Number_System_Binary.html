I'll discuss the conversion from binary to the other three base systems: decimal, octal, and hexadecimal.

  1. Conversion from Binary to Decimal (Base-2 to Base-10):

    To convert a binary number to decimal, you multiply each digit of the binary number by the corresponding power of 2 and then sum up the results.

    Example:

    Let's convert the binary number 1101 to decimal.

    • Step 1: Assign powers of 2 to each digit from right to left:

      1 * 23 + 1 * 22 + 0 * 21 + 1 * 20

    • Step 2: Perform the calculations:

      8 + 4 + 0 + 1 = 13

    Therefore, the binary number 1101 is equivalent to the decimal number 13.

  2. Conversion from Binary to Octal (Base-2 to Base-8):

    To convert a binary number to octal, you group the binary digits into sets of three starting from the rightmost digit. Then, convert each group to its corresponding octal digit.

    Example:

    Let's convert the binary number 101101 to octal.

    • Step 1: Group the binary digits into sets of three from right to left:

      10 110 1

    • Step 2: Convert each group to octal:

      10 (binary) = 2 (octal)

      110 (binary) = 6 (octal)

      1 (binary) = 1 (octal)

    Therefore, the binary number 101101 is equivalent to the octal number 261.

  3. Conversion from Binary to Hexadecimal (Base-2 to Base-16):

    To convert a binary number to hexadecimal, you group the binary digits into sets of four starting from the rightmost digit. Then, convert each group to its corresponding hexadecimal digit.

    Example:

    Let's convert the binary number 11011101 to hexadecimal

    • Step 1: Group the binary digits into sets of four from right to left:

      1101 1101

    • Step 2: Convert each group to hexadecimal:

      1101 (binary) = D (hexadecimal)

      1101 (binary) = D (hexadecimal)

    Therefore, the binary number 11011101 is equivalent to the hexadecimal number DD.

These examples demonstrate how to convert binary numbers to decimal, octal, and hexadecimal. Understanding these conversions is important in computer science, digital systems, and programming, where different base systems are commonly used.
